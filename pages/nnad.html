What is Digital Product Passport?

A digital identifier designed to trace the entire life cycle of a product. In the case of a garment, this digital ID is embodied by the QR code imprinted on the care label of the apparel.

Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR) is one of the key actions under the EU’s Circular Economy Action Plan (CEAP). Current EU legislation states all garments sold must have a physical care and content label to communicate product information, including washing instructions and material composition. Avery Dennison’s Digital Care Label takes the traditional care label one step further, providing information digitally, and helping brands prepare for coming legislation changes.

To gain a comprehensive understanding of how a garment ID could potentially track its recycling lifecycle, we conducted interviews with individuals working in various stages of the process, including garment stores, recycling stations, recycling sorting facilities, and end-recycle factories. Based on these interviews, we developed six personas representing the distinct roles and responsibilities within the garment recycling system. These personas will serve as invaluable guides in designing a product's digital ID that caters to the specific needs and requirements of each user group.

Part I - Build A Digital Care Label

The digital identifier on a garment is the QR code imprinted on the care label.

The QR code on the care label is a simple yet effective way to provide consumers with information about a garment. It's a great way to reduce fabric waste and to make it easier for consumers to care for their clothes properly. In addition to care instructions, the QR code could also be used to provide other information about the garment. Based on the interviews with end users and clients, we listed the information they are expecting to read:

  • The garment's origin and supply chain
  • The sustainability credentials of the materials used in the garment
  • Information about the garment's designer or brand
  • Translations in different languages

To determine the most preferred user interface design for the digital care label app, we created four distinct sets of wireframes and visual mockups. We then recruited over 30 users from four countries, dividing them into groups to test each design iteration. By analyzing user feedback and preferences, we aimed to identify the most appealing and user-friendly interface for our garment care digital label.

Based on the votes from interview groups, we decided to move on with one wireframe.

Part II - Build a Translation Plugin

Garment care labels are typically printed in multiple languages to ensure that consumers can understand the care instructions regardless of their native language. This is especially important for garments that are sold internationally.

Insights from User Researches

Providing digital garment care labels in multiple languages demonstrates a brand's commitment to customer satisfaction and global reach.
This can enhance brand perception and foster customer loyalty.

  • Translation Accuracy: Ensure that the translated care instructions are accurate and consistent with the original instructions to avoid confusion and potential garment damage.
  • Localization: Adapt the translated instructions to consider regional variations in washing practices, detergent use, and water temperature recommendations.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Be mindful of cultural sensitivities and avoid using language or imagery that could be misinterpreted or offensive in different regions.
  • Accessibility Features: Incorporate accessibility features such as text-to-speech and large font options to make the care instructions accessible to a wider audience.

Use what we have right now

We have a comprehensive database of care instruction translations, spanning an impressive array of 80 languages. This extensive repository serves as an invaluable resource for brands seeking to ensure that their garment care information is effectively communicated to a diverse and worldwide customer base.

Build a plugin for clients to edit translations

To streamline the translation process for our brand clients, we aim to develop a user-friendly interface that enables them to effortlessly select the care instruction content they want to translate from our database and identify any additional content that requires translation assistance. This intuitive interface will empower our clients to efficiently manage their translation needs, ensuring that their garment care instructions are accurately conveyed to a global audience.
